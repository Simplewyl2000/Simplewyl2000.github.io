MOpt: Optimized Mutation Scheduling for Fuzzers

  1. Abstract

    Mutation-based fuzzing is one of the most popular vulnerability discovery solutions. Its performance of generating interesting test cases highly depends on the mutation scheduling strategies. However, existing fuzzers usually follow a specific distribution to select mutation operators, which is inefficient in finding vulnerabilities on general programs. Thus, in this paper, we present a novel mutation scheduling scheme MOPT, which enables mutation-based fuzzers to discover vulnerabilities more efficiently. MOPT utilizes a customized Particle Swarm Optimization (PSO) algorithm to fifind the optimal selection probability distribution of operators with respect to fuzzing effectiveness, and provides a pacemaker fuzzing mode to accelerate the convergence speed of PSO. We applied MOPT to the state of-the-art fuzzers AFL, AFLFast and VUzzer, and implemented MOPT-AFL, -AFLFast and -VUzzer respectively, and then evaluated them on 13 real world open-source programs. The results showed that, MOPT-AFL could find 170% more security vulnerabilities and 350% more crashes than AFL. MOPT-AFLFast and MOPT-VUzzer also outperform their counterparts. Furthermore, the extensive evaluation also showed that MOPT provides a good rationality, compatibility and steadiness, while introducing negligible costs.

  1. The key problem targeted by the approach (a few sentences)

    Consider these several preconditions:

    1. Different mutation operators efficiencies on one target program are different.
    2. Each operators efficiency varies with target programs.
    3. AFL spends most time on the deterministic stage.
    4. AFL spends much time on the inefficient mutation operators

    Hence, the mutation schedulers in existing fuzzers, which follow some pre-defined distributions, are not efficient. Ideally, more time should be spent on mutation operators that perform better at generating interesting test cases. Therefore, a better mutation scheduler is demanded.

  1. Inputs of the approach (One sentence)

    Series of operators and target programs

  1. Outputs of the approach (One sentence)

    Selection probability distribution of the operators.

  1. Why the paper is accepted?

    In terms of usefulness, this approach has been used in several state-of-the-art fuzzers,including AFL, AFLFast and VUzzer, and evaluated them on 13 real world programs.

    The use of Particle Swarm Optimization to help determine how to arrange the operators is quite novel.

    This paper uses customized PSO algorithm, and it fits better in this scenario because it uses one swarm to represent one distribution, which corresponding to one solution, and it uses multiple swarms to represent multiple solutions, which makes full use of the group experience.

  1. Summary

    Chenyang Lyu et.al proposed a novel mutation scheduling scheme, named MOPT, based on Particle Swarm Optimization (PSO), which can efficiently determine the distribution of the mutation operators.

  1. Evaluation

    • The subject： Evaluated MOPT on 13 open-source linux programs , each of which comes from different source files and has different functionality representing a broad range of programs.

    • Tool used: AFL, Angora, VUzzer

    • Evaluation metric: The main evaluation metric is the number of the unique crashes discovered by each fuzzer. Since coverage-based fuzzers such as AFLFast and VUzzer consider that exploring more unique paths leads to more unique crashes, the second evaluation metric is the number of unique paths discovered by each fuzzer.

    • Key result: MOPT-AFL-tmp and MOPT-AFL-ever are significantly more efficient than AFL on all the programs. In total, MOPT-AFL-tmp and MOPT AFL-ever discover 2,195 and 2,334 more unique crashes than AFL on the 13 programs. Thus, MOPT-AFL has much better performance than AFL in exploring unique crashes. What’s more, Both MOPT-AFL-tmp and MOPT-AFL-ever discover more vulnerabilities than AFL by a wide margin. For instance, MOPT-AFL-tmp finds 45 more security CVEs than AFL; MOPT-AFL-ever finds 23 more unreported CVEs than AFL;

  1. Reflection

    1. In this paper, the cafeteria of choosing the operator is its efficiency of the creating interesting testing cases, however, it is a somehow heuristic idea. Because in the Havoc stage of the AFL, AFL selects a series of Ro mutation operators following the uniform distribution, and applies them on the seed to generate one test case, then the quality of the seed mutation can also be influenced by other operators, therefore, the consideration of the combination of these operators is needed.
    2. The experiments of the paper use only 13 real world program to test its feature, which may be not persuasive enough to prove its efficiency, more real world programs and scenarios are needed to test this new approach of mutation schedule.
    3. NN can also be used for seed choosing and mutation choosing, as for issues such as unbalanced data problem, it can be solved by https://www.usenix.org/conference/usenixsecurity20/presentation/zong
    4. Seed selection and mutation still have lots of interesting fields to explore.
